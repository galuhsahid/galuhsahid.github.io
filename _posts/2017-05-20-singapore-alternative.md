## Nanyang Old Coffee
I've said this countless times, but here's my favorite travel tip of all time: eat where the locals eat. This started out when I was in Paris a few years ago and I couldn't really afford expensive breakfasts... the prices seemed extraorbitant! So off we went to where the locals eat: at the Paris RER station, which turned out to be Paul, which turns out to be ridiculously expensive in Jakarta. 

I've been clinging to said travel tip ever since, so we had our lunch at one of the lowkey food courts in a mall in Tiong Bahru like a regular citizen. But the most special was a breakfast at Nanyang Old Coffee in Chinatown. It was one of the very few places that was open from 7am, so a lot of locals stopped by too before they went to work or school. I had a *nasi lemak* &amp; a cup of coffee, which were enough to last me for a day. Hashtag cheapskate. ðŸ˜€
